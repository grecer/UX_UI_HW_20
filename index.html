Nonprofit Website Redesign

Making a nonprofit website responsive and more accessible to users through a full UX Redesign.

Team of 5, project delivered in 3 weeks

Role: Researcher, UX Designer

Deliverables:
  • Slide Deck containing research results and design process
  • Finished Figma prototype for both desktop and mobile versions of the website
Tools:
  • Figma
  • Google Suite
  • Slack
  • Pinterest
  • Procreate

The Problem:

The current Gifts for Seniors website was outdated and didnâ€™t have a global navbar making it difficult for new users and potential volunteers to use their site.

Our Solution:

Create a navbar and style guide to make the website intuitive and cohesive, making sure important information to users such as mission statement and volunteer opportunities are prioritized.

Research

  1. Logo - Limited Changes.
  2. Nav Bars - Top nav bar is overshadowed by row of buttons below. Row of buttons is not only inconsistent, but also not very helpful for navigation.
  3. Hero Image - Low quality and odd aspect ratio.
  4. Call to Action - Effective for driving donations, but space/copy could be utilized better.
  5. Testimonials - Personal insights from previous volunteers are very beneficial to new-comers.
  1. Images Carousel - Images are low quality.
  2. Footer - Broken links, could be sorted more clearly.
